While I was working at Siemens, I was given an opportunity to participate the company publication: Codimensional Optimization of Differential Padstacks. As the primary author of the publication, I was in charge of conducting experiments and simulate optimization on models using HyperLynx. It was awarded as the best paper from the EPEPS IEEE comittee. I simulated single and multi-processes of electronic package designs in parallel by applying optimization and sampling techniques to find parameter sensitivity and an optimal package design. Simultaneous codimensional optimization to differential via padstack designs is proposed to optimize a single padstack definition so that it can handle multiple entry/exit layer transitions

Data Visualization

I was given a 1024 by 1024 electrical performance dataset to conduct statistical analysis on the dataset. Because surface roughness has a significant influence on electrical performance, I investigated this dataset in both qualitative and quantitative analysis. I came up with a 3D and 2D model of the dataset and provided the representation to the vendors. The 3D visualization allowed the vendors to have an understanding of qualitative analysis of checking data validity and consistency, and statistical analysis was applied on the 2D visualization to perform quantitative analysis. The vendor could get the information that the data does not appear to follow a Gaussian distribution and appeared to be multi-modal with deviation. Therefore, I suggested the vendor further fitness test using Chi-Square test, Kolmogorov-Smirnov test for validation and distribution assumption.
